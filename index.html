A Visitor's Guide to Hawaii's Waikoloa Village

Where are we located?

Waikoloa Village is located on the northwest side of the Big Island of Hawaii. As seen by the Google Map below on the left, the region is bordered by Hawaii Route 19; driving down this breathtaking highway route provides an exclusive glimpse into the unique lava terrain on the Kona coast of Hawaii.

A majority of Waikoloa is immersed in volcanic remnants left from the 1859 Mauna Loa eruption. An exciting pasttime for tourists is partaking in some natural graffiti with the white coral rocks that are scattered throughout the coast (see above image on the right).

What is there to do?

The amount of activties and adventures to have in this part of the Big Island is countless. From beach exploring to fine dining, to hiking and helicopter riding, there are almost too many options for visitors to choose from!
